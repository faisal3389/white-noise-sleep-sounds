# White Noise — TikTok Playbook (Week 1)

> **Format:** Faceless, no voiceover. Premium app visuals + stock cozy loops + text overlays + trending audio + App Store CTA.
> **Goal for Week 1:** Ship 14 videos (2/day, 7 days). Learn what hooks land. Build the muscle, not chase virality.

---

## PART 1 — One-Time Setup (Do This Once, ~90 Minutes Total)

### 1a. Record your app (30 min)

Turn on iOS Screen Recording (Settings → Control Center → Screen Recording). Open the app fresh each time so the animations look clean. Record these **12 clips**, 5-10 seconds each, in portrait. Save to a dedicated album called "WhiteNoise-Raw".

| # | What to Record | Duration | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | App launch — opening from home screen, splash, landing | 5s | Cinematic opener |
| 2 | Category grid — slow scroll through all categories | 8s | Shows variety, beautiful UI |
| 3 | Tapping a Rain sound → it plays, waveform animates | 6s | Core product moment |
| 4 | Now Playing screen — full screen with sound playing, visuals | 10s | The "hero" shot |
| 5 | Volume slider — slow drag up and down | 4s | Satisfying ASMR interaction |
| 6 | Sleep timer — setting 30 min, countdown appears | 6s | Feature highlight |
| 7 | Mix screen — adding 2-3 sounds together, adjusting levels | 10s | Premium-feeling feature |
| 8 | Loop mode toggle — showing the loop icon lighting up | 3s | Small detail flex |
| 9 | Premium paywall — opening it, slow scroll showing all tiers | 8s | For the CTA video |
| 10 | Fire sounds playing — showing the warm-themed background | 6s | Mood variety |
| 11 | Nature sounds playing — showing the green-themed background | 6s | Mood variety |
| 12 | Swiping between 3-4 sounds rapidly | 5s | Dopamine hit |

**Tip:** Record each clip twice — one "normal speed" and one "slow" version. You'll use slow versions for moody/dreamy edits. Trim in Photos app before moving to CapCut.

### 1b. Download free cozy stock loops (15 min)

Go to **Pexels.com** (free, no login needed, royalty-free). Search and download these loops — download the vertical / portrait versions when available:

- "rain on window" (5-10 variants — these are your bread and butter)
- "fireplace loop"
- "cozy bedroom night"
- "forest mist morning"
- "starry night sky"
- "lofi room aesthetic"
- "candle flickering"
- "ocean waves night"
- "city rain night"
- "thunderstorm window"

Also grab from **Pixabay.com**:
- Search "ASMR" — tons of slow, atmospheric loops
- Search "aesthetic" — Gen Z-style cozy content
- Search "dark academia" — moody, premium-looking visuals

Save everything to an album called "WhiteNoise-BRoll". You now have a lifetime's worth of B-roll.

### 1c. Install CapCut and set up (10 min)

Download **CapCut** from the App Store (free). Open it once, create an account (or sign in with Apple). In Settings, turn off "CapCut watermark" — on iOS this is automatic when you export, just make sure the final output doesn't have it.

### 1d. Save your "template" values (5 min)

You'll use the same styling for every video. Write these down in Notes:
- **Font:** Komika Axis or Montserrat Bold (both in CapCut's free library)
- **Color:** White text with 3-4pt black stroke/outline
- **Position:** Top 1/4 of screen for hook, bottom 1/3 for body text
- **Animation:** "Rise" or "Typewriter" for hook, "Pop" for body text
- **Size:** 80-100pt for hook, 50-60pt for body

This consistency is how you build visual brand recognition.

### 1e. Find your go-to trending audio (15 min)

Open TikTok. Search these tags and save (bookmark) 5-10 trending sounds:
- "sleep aesthetic"
- "lofi sleep"
- "cozy vibes"
- "rainy night"
- "ASMR"

Look for sounds with **rising usage** (TikTok shows this as "trending up"). Sounds under 100k uses are better than ones over 1M — less saturated.

### 1f. Screen Record your app once with a "beauty reel" mindset (15 min)

Record ONE single 60-second beauty reel: you opening the app, scrolling categories slowly, playing a sound, opening Now Playing, showing volume/timer/mix — but done slowly, smoothly, like a commercial. This becomes your universal B-roll for any video where you need "app footage."

## PART 2 — CapCut Walkthrough (First Video, Step-by-Step)

Once. After this, each video takes 5-8 min.

**1. Create New Project.** Tap "+" → "New Project" → select your 3-4 clips for this video (mix of app recording + stock loops).

**2. Set canvas to 9:16.** Bottom toolbar → "Format" → choose "9:16". This is TikTok vertical.

**3. Layer your clips.** Drag your clips onto the timeline in the order you want. Trim each one by tapping the clip and dragging the edges.

**4. Add a background layer (optional but recommended).** Tap "Overlay" → "Add overlay" → drop a cozy stock loop as the base layer. Your app recording goes on top, slightly smaller, so the cozy visual shows around the edges. Shrink the app clip to ~80% scale.

**5. Add hook text (first 2 seconds).**
- Tap "Text" → "Add text"
- Type your hook line (from the scripts below)
- Font: Komika Axis or Montserrat Bold, white, stroke 4pt black
- Drag it to the top 1/4 of the screen
- Tap the clock icon next to the text → set duration to 2-3 seconds
- Tap "Animation" → "In" → "Rise" → "Out" → "Fade out"

**6. Add body text (mid-video).**
- Same process, but place text in middle or bottom 1/3
- Keep each line on screen 1.5-2.5 seconds
- Stagger so only one text layer is visible at a time

**7. Add the CTA ending card.**
- At the last 3 seconds, add text: "White Noise" + "Free on the App Store" + "🔗 in bio"
- Use your accent color (cyan #06b6d4 matches your app) for the app name
- Fade in, hold, fade out

**8. Add trending audio.**
- Tap "Audio" → "Sounds" → search your saved trending sounds
- Drop one in, trim to match your video length
- Lower volume to 70% if it's too loud

**9. Export.**
- Top right "Export" button
- Resolution: 1080p
- Frame rate: 30fps
- Quality: Recommended
- Save to Photos

**10. Post to TikTok.**
- Open TikTok → tap "+"
- Select your exported video
- Skip the TikTok editor (you already edited)
- Caption: re-type your hook as the first line, then 3-5 hashtags
- **Don't** add another sound — you already have one
- Post time: aim for 9pm your local time for sleep content (peak audience)

## PART 7 — What to Track

After 7 days, note for each video:
- Views (especially the ratio: views-per-follower at post time)
- Likes
- Saves (most important — saves = algorithm boost)
- Shares
- Comments
- Profile visits
- **App Store conversion** — check App Store Connect "Web Referrer" → TikTok

The 2-3 highest performers? Make 3 variants of each next week. That's how you find your format.

## One Final Honest Note

The first 3-5 videos will probably get 100-500 views each. That's normal. Do not quit. The algorithm takes 5-15 posts to learn who to show your account to. The accounts that hit their stride almost always look dead for the first 2 weeks.

The goal of week 1 isn't views. It's **finding the hook that makes someone screenshot and share**. Track the comments carefully — the phrases people repeat back to you are the hooks that work. Those phrases go on top of videos in week 2.
